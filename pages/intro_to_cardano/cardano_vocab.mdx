# Cardano Vocab to Know

### ADA
ADA is the primary currency of the Cardano network. It is used for various purposes such as making transactions, interacting with decentralized applications (Dapps), staking, and voting. 

It is named after Ada Lovelace, the 19th-century mathematician who is recognized as the first computer programmer.

### Lovelace 
ADA can be divided into smaller units called Lovelace, similar to how a dollar is divided into cents. One Lovelace is the smallest possible unit of ADA. 

One ADA is equivalent to 1,000,000 Lovelace. Inversely, one Lovelace is equal to 0.000001 ADA.

### Founding Entities 
The 3 founding entities are the organizations cooperate to build, grow, and market the Cardano blockchain. They each received part of the [initial distribution](https://cardano.org/genesis/) of Cardano.  

##### [Input Output](https://iohk.io/en/) 
This is the organization run by Cardano's founder Charles Hoskinson. Input Output is behind in the foundational research and software development of the Cardano blockchain. They partner with a network of academic institutions to determine the proper design, and then implement it using open-source software engineering. 

##### [Cardano Foundation](https://cardanofoundation.org/)
The Cardano Foundation is an independent Swiss-based non-profit that oversees and supervises the advancement of Cardano. They are the legal custodian of the Cardano brand and work with regulators in different jurisdictions to shape blockchain legislation. They also work with the community to help build open-source tools that can be leveraged by builders. 

##### [Emurgo](https://www.emurgo.io/)
Emurgo is the official commercial arm of Cardano. They have different business units such as Fintech for financial infrastructure, Media to produce and source great content, Ventures to invest in promising Cardano companies, Middle East & Africa which is focused on regional adoption, and the Emurgo Academy which provides Cardano education.  

Read more about the founding entities [here](https://cardano.org/partners/?tab=iohk#partners-section).
